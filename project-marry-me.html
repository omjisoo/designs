ABOUT

project marry me is an interactive space for individual contemplation and community conversation on self-love. The private self-guided ceremony is modeled after the western wedding ceremony, in which the participant recites the vows of self-love. The three sections of the ceremony mirror three stages of the flower life cycle as the metaphor of personal growth.

The space includes soft carpets (to be experienced without shoes), dark green velvet curtains, and round-edged furniture to create a calming and safe environment. It is versatile for both private meditation and community gatherings.

Upon entering the Reception Hall, you are invited to contemplate as an individual and as a community on what self-love truly means. Printed materials are available for study in the shared area.

You can also take an invitation card which holds information about the project and vows of self-love to recite and/or sign.

The ceremony is broken down into three steps:



1. Walk the Aisle: The seed endures for a long time, holding onto its potential.

2. Say the Vows: The bud looks within itself and prepares to join the world in conversation.

3. Tie the Knot: The bloom delights everyone with its existence and continues onto another cycle of life.



The cyclical nature of the ceremony means that you can come back again and again in times of emotional unrest.

Once you’re ready to proceed with the ceremony, the first step is to walk the Aisle (The Seed). At first you walk alone in complete darkness, until the light gradually comes back on as you ascend to the next stage.

In the Ceremony Room (The Bud), you renew the vows of self-love in all of your presence, with your past behind you and future in front of you. This will be an entirely private moment to yourself to practice mindfulness.

When you exit the Ceremony Room, you step back into the Reception Hall. Here you tie a flower knot (The Bloom) on the rope to mark this celebratory moment in your life and show your support for self-love.
