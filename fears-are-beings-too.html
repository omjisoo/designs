It started with little doodles back in 2019 in which I gave a face and a voice to each of my fears as these supernatural beings. The visualized conversations ended up being very profound so I decided to expand on it as an Independent Study project for the semester.

Storyboards were used to determine the flow of the book.

Character sketches: Eye Contact, Conversation, Body Weight, Awkward Silence.

In the beginning of the book, I look visibly tense because I don't know what to expect from my fears. Once I get to know them, however, I become more comfortable. The bare toes also signify being open and vulnerable to the inner conversations.

Fears Are Beings Too

Print, Illustration, 2019

Fears Are Beings Too is a book of conversation and reconciliation with my own fears.

The book features eight subjects I fear of: Eye Contact, Conversation, Body Weight, Belonging Nowhere, Asking for Help, Awkward Silence, Rejection, and Being Honest. After years of shunning the fears, I finally sit down and have a heart-to-heart talk with each one to understand why they are still with me. In the process, I discover that they have much insight to offer.

The first edition was produced in limited quantities by hand with coptic stitch binding and letterpressed hardcovers. Currently I'm looking for a way to publish a second edition that will be available for more widespread distribution.
