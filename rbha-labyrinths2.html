RBHA Meditation Labyrinths

⮑ Project ⮑ Process

RBHA Meditation Labyrinths are a set of two labyrinths designed for the residents on recovery at the RBHA facility. The project reimagines a section of the Healing Garden as a space of reflection and relaxation. Under the direction of mOb studio, an interdisciplinary design program, our team continued the work of the previous team by incorporating the imagery of a necklace connecting different elements of the garden together.



client

Richmond Behavioral Health Authority


co-designer

Gabrielle Stadulis


category

Experience


year

2018

The shape of the labyrinth was inspired by the natural labyrinth within the human ear. It also refers to the breath mark in music which gives the instrumentalist or the singer a breathing room in between measures.



Residents and visitors may navigate through the labyrinths in a winding fashion, with finger labyrinths available at each station for those with restricted mobility.

Our team came up with a site plan and a 1:120 scale model of the labyrinths to showcase at the end of spring.
