ABOUT

Intercultural Festival (ICF) is an annual festival celebrating cultural diversity at Virginia Commonwealth University and the Richmond city. As the graphic designer and member of the ICF Executive Board, I explored a variety of styles to advertise each event with a distinct theme. From 2018 to 2019, I also served as the Assistant Director and paid special attention in crafting meaningful experiences for the visitors.



category

Experience, Print


year

2017—2019

Following the theme of ICF 2017, Threads of Cultural Diversity, I illustrated the globe as a ball of colorful interwoven cultures. This design was applied across various media such as posters, booklets, bus ads, t-shirts, and Snapchat filters.

Even though this proposed poster for ICF 2018 was not approved, the marketing team based the final visuals on this concept.

The ICF Executive Board also coordinated smaller events throughout the year to promote cross-cultural exchanges. In creating social media images for the events, I made an effort to convey the culture(s) represented.

For ICF 2019, I proposed Love, World as the theme, centering around the idea of messages traveling across the globe. As part of the campaign, four different artists (including myself) designed postcards depicting a diverse mix of terrains and cultures. We then distributed the postcards with questions for the visitors to answer on what global citizenship means to them.
