project marry me

⮑ Project ⮑ Process

project marry me is an interactive space for individual contemplation and community conversation on self-love. The private self-guided ceremony is modeled after the western wedding ceremony, in which the participant recites the vows of self-love. The three sections of the ceremony mirror three stages of the flower life cycle as the metaphor of personal growth.



category

Experience, Print


year

2019

The space includes soft carpets (to be experienced without shoes), dark green velvet curtains, and round-edged furniture to create a calming and safe environment. It is versatile for both private meditation and community gatherings.

Upon entering the Reception Hall, you are invited to contemplate as an individual and as a community on what self-love truly means. Printed materials are available for study in the shared area.

You can also take an invitation card which holds information about the project and vows of self-love to recite and sign.

The ceremony of self-love is broken down into three steps:

① Walk the Aisle – "The Seed"

Once you’re ready to proceed with the ceremony, the first step is to walk the Aisle. At first you walk alone in complete darkness, until the light gradually comes back on as you ascend to the next stage.

② Say the Vows – "The Bud"

In the Ceremony Room, you renew the vows of self-love in all of your presence, with your past behind you and future in front of you within the double mirrors. This will be an entirely private moment to yourself to practice mindfulness.

③ Tie the Knot – "The Bloom"

When you exit the Ceremony Room, you step back into the Reception Hall. Here you tie a flower knot on the rope to mark this celebratory moment in your life and show your support for self-love.
